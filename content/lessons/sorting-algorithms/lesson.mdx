<Intuition>
Sorting is the drosophila of algorithms: every big idea — divide and
conquer, randomization, adversarial inputs, time/space trades, lower
bounds — shows up here first. Race the three below on the same array. Then
feed the race an **already-sorted** input and watch naive-pivot quicksort,
the average-case hero, degrade to bubble-sort pace while merge sort doesn't
blink. Adversarial inputs are why "average case" is a bet, not a promise.
</Intuition>
