<Intuition>
A heap is a tree that gave up everything except one promise — **the parent
is never worse than its children** — and in exchange got to live inside a
flat array with no pointers at all. The index math IS the tree: children of
i at 2i+1 and 2i+2. The visual shows both views side by side because the
duality is the lesson: what looks like "swap with parent" on the tree is
"swap two array slots" in memory. This is how your OS scheduler and Uber
dispatch keep "most important next" one array-read away.
</Intuition>
