<Intuition>
Arrays make you pay to rearrange; linked lists make rearrangement a
three-pointer shuffle — and make you pay for everything else. The list
earns its place in exactly one situation: **you're already holding the node
you care about**, and you want to splice in O(1). That's browser history,
undo chains, and the eviction end of every LRU cache. Below: reversal, the
canonical pointer surgery. The bug you will write someday — overwriting a
pointer you hadn't read yet — is drawn in red when it would happen.
</Intuition>
