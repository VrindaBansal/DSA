<Intuition>
Most production "AI agents" aren't agents at all — they're **workflows**:
systems where LLM calls are composed through control flow *you* wrote. That
distinction (Anthropic's framing) is the most clarifying idea in the whole
agentic space. Workflows are predictable, testable, cheap, and debuggable
because the path is fixed in code; agents hand that control to the model,
buying flexibility at the cost of all four. The craft is composing a handful of
reliable patterns — chaining, routing, parallelization, orchestrator-worker,
evaluator-optimizer — and adding genuine autonomy *only* at the steps where the
path truly can't be known ahead of time. Determinism is a feature; spend
autonomy like money.
</Intuition>
