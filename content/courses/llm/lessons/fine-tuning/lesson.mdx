<Intuition>
Fine-tuning is the most powerful and most *over-reached-for* lever in the
toolkit. The instinct — "the model doesn't know our stuff, let's fine-tune it
on our docs" — is usually wrong, because fine-tuning teaches *behavior* (tone,
format, a narrow skill), not *facts* (which go stale the moment you freeze them
and can't be cited — that's RAG's job). The decision order is
prompt → few-shot → RAG → fine-tune, cheapest first. When you do fine-tune,
**LoRA** is the trick that made it affordable: freeze the giant base model and
train tiny low-rank adapter matrices — most of the benefit for a fraction of
the compute, and swappable per task. The right mental model: fine-tune HOW the
model responds, retrieve WHAT it says, and prompt for everything you can get
away with.
</Intuition>

<Concept>
## RAG vs fine-tuning for "make it know X"

The comparison that settles most arguments: changing/citable knowledge is RAG;
consistent behavior/format is fine-tuning. They compose.
</Concept>
