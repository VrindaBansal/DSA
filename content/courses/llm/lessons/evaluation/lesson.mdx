<Intuition>
"It seems better" is not a measurement, and vibes don't survive contact with
production. Evaluation is what turns LLM development from guesswork into
engineering: a **golden set** of input→expected pairs is your regression suite,
and every prompt tweak, model swap, or retrieval change becomes a diff against
it. Two ideas do most of the work. **LLM-as-judge** scales human grading — a
model scores outputs against a rubric — but it inherits real biases (it favors
longer answers, the first option shown, and its own family's style), so you
calibrate it against human labels. And for RAG you must measure retrieval and
generation *separately* — faithfulness, context precision/recall — or you'll
spend a week "fixing the prompt" when the bug was retrieval. Quality is nothing
without cost and latency next to it.
</Intuition>
